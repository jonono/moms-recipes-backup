Asian Persuasion Pork Loin

10 to 12

INGREDIENTS

1 1/2 cups dry red wine
1/2 cup brown sugar
1/2 cup ketchup
1/2 cup water
1/4 cup soy sauce
2 Tbsp. oil
2 tsp. curry powder
1 tsp. ground ginger
1/2 tsp. garlic powder
1/2 tsp. ground pepper
4 lb. boneless pork loin roast
1 1/2 cups water
1 Tbsp. cornstarch

DIRECTIONS

1. Combine first 10 ingredients (wine through pepper).   Measure out 2 cups of wine mixture and reserve for making sauce.  Refrigerate.  Pour remaining wine mixture into a large resealable plastic bag to use as marinade.  Add roast to bag and coat with marinade.  Seal bag, place on tray and refrigerate turning bag occasionally, 24 hours.
2. Remove from fridge and let stand 20 to 30 minutes.  Line a shallow roasting pan with foil.  Remove roast from marinade and discard marinade.  Place roast on rack in 425 preheated oven for 30 minutes.  Pour 1 1/2 cups water into pan.  Reduce temperature to 350 and continue roasting for 1 hour.
3. Meanwhile, place cornstarch in a med saucepan.  Gradually whisk in reserved wine mixture and bring to boil over med heat, stirring constantly.  Cook until thickened about 2 minutes and remove from heat.
4. Measure out 2 Tbsp. sauce and reserve.  Use remaining sauce to serve with roast.  Remove pan from oven and baste roast with reserved sauce.  Continue roasting for 15 minutes or until meat thermometer rads 160.  Transfer roast to a platter and cover with foil 15 minutes before carving.  Reheat remaining sauce over low heat.
