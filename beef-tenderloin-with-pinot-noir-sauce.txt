Beef Tenderloin with Pinot Noir Sauce

6 to 8

INGREDIENTS

1/3 cup balsamic vinegar
1/3 cup olive oil
2 Tbsp. fresh chopped parsley
2 cloves garlic, chopped
2 tsp. basil crumbled
2 tsp. oregano crumbled
2 lb. beef tenderloin roast
Sauce
1 cup chopped onion
1 cup chopped carrots
1 cup chopped celery
1 Tbsp. butter
2 Tbsp. tomato paste
1/2 tsp. ground pepper
1 1/4 cups Pinot Noir or dry red wine
1 can beef broth
1 can chicken broth
2 Tbsp. butter, softened
1 Tbsp. flour
1/4 tsp. pepper

DIRECTIONS

1. Combine marinade ingredients (vinegar through oregano) in resealable plastic bag.  Add beef and squeeze bag to coat.  Seal and place on plate.  Refrigerate up to 24 hours turning bag occasionally.  Remove beef from bag and  pat dry.  Discard marinade.  Grill over med heat until desired doneness.  Let stand 5 minutes before carving and serve with Pinot Noir Sauce below.
2. Saute onion, carrots and celery in fry pan about 5 minutes in 1 Tbsp. butter.  Stir in tomato paste and 1/2 tsp. pepper.  Add wine and boil until reduced by half, about 5  minutes.  Add beef broth and chicken broth and boil until reduced to 1 1/2 cups.  Strain and discards solids.  Return strained liquid to fry pan and bring to simmer.  Combine softened butter with flour and whisk into simmering liquid.  Cook, stirring frequently until thickened, about 1 minute.  Stir in 1 tsp. pepper.  Serve with beef.
